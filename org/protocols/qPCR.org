#+OPTIONS: toc:nil
#+TITLE: Reverse Transcription and qPCR
* Reagents
- qScript Ultra SuperMix (Quanta Cat# 95217)
- PowerUP SYBR Green Master Mix (Applied Biosystems Cat# A25742)
- RNAase-free water
* Notes
- This protocol uses a scaled-down 10 µL reaction volume (half of the
  manufacturer's recommendation). A 10 µL cDNA reaction is typically
  sufficient for testing up to 18 genes by qPCR.
- Use 200 ng to 1 µg of input RNA (typically ~0.5 µg).
- Measure RNA concentration using a NanoDrop spectrophotometer.
* Procedure
** cDNA Synthesis
- Reaction Composition (10 µL Total Volume)
  1. Input RNA: X µL
  2. Quanta mix: 2 µL
  3. RNase-free water: (10 - 2 - X) µL

- Reverse Transcription Program
  1. 25°C for 2 minutes
  2. 55°C for 10 minutes
  3. 95°C for 1 minute
  4. Hold at 4°C

- Tips
  1. cDNA may be stored at -20°C before proceeding to qPCR.
  2. Add water first in the mix and rinse everything else (RNA and
     cDNA enzyme mix) into the water

** cDNA Dilution
- Example dilution (12 Samples)
  1. Prepare a 1:5 standard by combining 2 µL from each RT reaction
     (24 µL total) with 96 µL water to obtain 120 µL.
  2. Prepare a 1:10 standard by mixing 40 µL of the 1:5 standard with
     40 µL water.
  3. Prepare 1:50 and 1:100 standards by diluting the 1:5 and 1:10
     standards, respectively (8 µL sample + 72 µL water).
  4. Similarly, prepare 1:500 and 1:1000 standards from the 1:50 and
     1:100 standards.
     - You should obtain approximately 72 µL of each standard dilution
       from 1:5 through 1:1000.
  5. Dilute samples 1:10 so they fall within the standard curve range.
     - Example: dilute 7.5 µL stock cDNA to 75 µL total volume (67.5
       µL water).
  6. Because housekeeping genes (e.g., 18S rRNA or β-actin) are highly expressed, prepare 1:100 dilutions from the 1:10 samples:
     - 1.5 µL of 1:10 diluted cDNA + 13.5 µL water.
- Notes and tips
  1. Proper dilution is essential for efficient PCR amplification.
  2. The dilution step is intended to generate:
     - Serially diluted standards (pooled from all samples).
     - Diluted samples for target genes (typically 1:10 dilution).
     - Diluted samples for housekeeping genes (e.g., 18S rRNA; typically 1:100 dilution).
  3. Above is just a simplified dilution procedure. To maximize the
     number of genes can be tested, dilute the cDNA as below.

** qPCR Plate Setup
- Primers are typically ordered from IDT in lyophilized form.
  - Resuspend to 100 µM (e.g., 18.1 nmol in 181 µL water).
  - Dilute 1:10 to prepare a 10 µM working solution.
- Prepare a master mix containing SYBR reagent and primers.
- Add 17 µL of prepared master mix to each well, followed by 3 µL of diluted sample or standard.

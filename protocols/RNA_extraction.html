RNA Extraction with TRIzol

Reagents

  • TRIzol reagent (invitrogen: Cat# 15596018)
  • Isopropanol
  • 75% Ethanol
  • RNAase free water

Notes

  • Use RNAase free solutions and tubes
  • Perform all steps at room temperature unless otherwise noted
  • Optimal amount of input tissue/cell (for 1mL TRIzol)
    • 5-10x106 cells (10cm dish)
    • 30–80 mg of tissue
      • Mouse Kidney: 40-50 mg
      • Mouse liver: 20-30 mg
    • Sample volume should not exceed 25% of the volume of TRIzol

Procedures

  1. Tissue/cell processing
    • Trypsinize cells and wash twice in PBS
    • Tissue homognization
      • Pre-chill a clean mortar and pestle on dry ice
      • Slowly grind the tissue into a fine powder
      • Transfer optimal amount of the powder into pre-chilled tubes
      • Add 1 mL of TRIzol to the powder
      • Pass the lysate through a 20G needle, then a 23G needle, 10 times each
        • This step might be optional.
  2. Incubate tissue/cell in TRIzol solution for 5 min
    • The solution may turn viscous due to DNA content
  3. Add 200 μL chloroform for every mL of TRIzol. Shake the samples vigorously for 15 sec to mix (do not vortex). Incubate for 2-3 min
  4. Centrifuge samples 15 min. at 12, 000xg at 4℃. Centrifuging cold yields better phase separation
    • The mixture separates into a lower phenol-chloroform, an interphase, and a colorless upper aqueous phase. RNA is in the aqueous phase.
  5. Transfer the aqueous phase containing the RNA to a new tube
    • Glycogen can be added to the aqueous phase for small amount of input to help visualize the pellet
  6. Precipitate the RNA by mixing with 0.5 ml of isopropanol for every ml of TRIzol used initially
  7. Mix tube by inverting and incubate for 10 minutes
  8. Centrifuge for 10 min at 12,000xg, at 4℃.
    • Discard supernatant
  9. Wash the RNA pellet with 1ml. 75% Ethanol for every ml TRIzol used. Vortex the sample briefly, then centrifuge for 5 minutes at 7500 × g at 4°C.
    • Discard the supernatant
    • The wash can be repeated to futher remove residual guanidine thiocyanate
  10. Vacuum or air dry the RNA pellet for 5–10 minutes
    • Do not overdry the RNA pellet. (When sufficiently dried, the pelet should appear whitish. If pelet is clear, it indicates that it has been overdried).
  11. Resuspend the pellet in 30-200 μL of RNase-free water.
    • 150-200 μL for tissue
    • 30-50 μL for cells
    • Adjust depending on the size of pellet.
  12. Incubate in a water bath or heat block set at 55–60°C for 10–15 minutes.

TIPS: There are 3 steps where the RNA isolation can be interrupted and RNA can be stored until further isolation. However, try to isolate it in one step to avoid degradation.

  1. After adding TRIzol and before adding chloroform. Store at –70℃ up to a few months (see step 3 above).
  2. After adding Isopropanol to the aqueous phase. Store it at 4℃, only overnight. Do not store any colder because the salts will precipitate out (see step 7 above).
  3. After precipitation and adding 75% Ethanol. In 75% Ethanol, RNA can be stored up to a week at 4℃ or a few months at –20℃ (see step 10 above).

Immunofluorescence Staining with OCT Tissue

Overview

This protocol describes indirect immunofluorescence staining of PFA-fixed, OCT-embedded frozen tissue sections (validated on mouse kidney).

Safety notes

  • Acetone is volatile and flammable; fix slides in a fume hood or well-ventilated area, away from open flame.
  • Sudan Black B is a suspected carcinogen and an irritant; handle with gloves and prepare/apply in a fume hood.

Materials/Equipment

  • Humidified chamber
  • Hydrophobic barrier pen
  • Coplin jars or slide staining dishes
  • Fume hood (for acetone fixation and Sudan Black step)

Reagents

  1. Acetone (-20°C)
  2. Phosphate-buffered saline (PBS)
  3. Blocking buffer: 1x PBS with 5% goat serum and 2% BSA
  4. Sudan Black B (0.05% in 70% ethanol) — filter before use to remove precipitate; light-sensitive, store protected from light.
  5. DAPI (Invitrogen Cat#D1306)

Procedures

  1. Remove slides from storage (-80°C) and allow to warm to room temperature for 5 min.
  2. Fix the slides in -20°C acetone. Incubate for 10 min, then air dry until the acetone has fully evaporated.
  3. Wash the slides twice in 1x PBS, 5 min each.
  4. Dry the area around the tissue and apply a ring of hydrophobic barrier. Allow the barrier to dry for 1 min.
  5. Block the slides with blocking buffer. Incubate in a humidified chamber for 1 hour.
    • Use about 100 μL per kidney section.
    • Decant the blocking buffer after 1 hour.
  6. Without washing, apply diluted primary antibody to the tissue.
    • Incubate in a humidified chamber at 4°C overnight.
    • Protect from light if using conjugated antibodies.
    • Need about 50 μL per mouse kidney section.
  7. Decant primary antibody solution.
  8. Wash with 1x PBS two times, 5 min each.
  9. Apply diluted secondary antibody. Incubate 1 hour at room temperature in a humidified chamber, protected from light.
    • Secondary antibodies are usually used at 1:500 dilution.
  10. Wash with 1x PBS three times, 5 min each, protected from light.
  11. Optional: DAPI staining
    • The mounting media contains DAPI, but additional staining may produce more uniform DAPI signal.
    • Stock solution: 1 mg/mL (2.86 mM) in DI/MQ water.
    • Dilute 2.86 mM stock to 300 μM: 31.47 μL DAPI + 268.53 μL PBS.
    • Dilute the 300 μM intermediate solution 1:1000 in PBS to achieve 1x working concentration.
    • Incubate for 5 min, protected from light.
    • Wash with 1x PBS twice, 5 min each, before proceeding.
  12. Block endogenous autofluorescence with 0.05% Sudan Black B diluted in 70% EtOH. Incubate for 10 minutes at room temperature, protected from light.
  13. Wash slides twice with DI water, 10 min each.
  14. Mount and seal slides.

  2. Always include a negative control when possible.
    • Pick the sample expected to have positive signal (i.e., an isotype or secondary-only control run alongside a known-positive tissue).
  3. When applying liquid, avoid pipetting directly onto the tissue, as this may cause detachment. Instead, slowly pipet the liquid along the side of the tissue section.
